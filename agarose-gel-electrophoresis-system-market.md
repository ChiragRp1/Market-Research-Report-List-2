<p><p>Agarose gel electrophoresis system is a technique used to separate and analyze DNA fragments and proteins based on their size and charge. Agarose, a polysaccharide extracted from seaweed, is used to create a gel matrix where samples are loaded and subjected to an electric field. The gel is porous, allowing smaller molecules to migrate faster than larger ones, resulting in distinct bands that can be visualized using various staining methods. This technique is widely used in molecular biology research, genetic testing, forensics, and clinical diagnostics.</p><p>The future of the agarose gel electrophoresis market looks promising with a projected CAGR of 6.1% during the forecasted period. Tape Style systems use precast gel tapes that are placed in a cassette, offering convenience and ease of use. Dam Style systems employ a gel-casting DAM that creates a gel in a disposable cassette, and the electrophoresis process takes place within the cassette.</p></p>
