<p><p>Image processing systems can be categorized into two main types: digital and analog. Digital image processing systems deal with representing and manipulating images using numbers, algorithms, and computer software. They enable high precision, flexibility, and a wide range of image enhancement and analysis techniques. On the other hand, analog image processing systems involve processing images as continuous electrical signals. They rely on dedicated hardware components and are often limited in terms of flexibility and computational capabilities compared to their digital counterparts.</p></p>
