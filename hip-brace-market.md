<p><p>In the hip brace market, there are two main types: fixed hip braces and wearable hip braces. Fixed hip braces are designed to immobilize the hip joint and provide maximum stability and support. They are typically made of rigid materials and are suitable for post-operative recovery or severe hip injuries. On the other hand, wearable hip braces are more flexible and allow for a wider range of motion. They provide support and compression to the hip area, making them ideal for hip pain relief, injury prevention, or mild to moderate hip conditions.</p></p>
