<p><p>Self-regulating thermostatic radiator valves are devices used to regulate the temperature of individual radiators in a heating system. They are available in two main types: self-operate thermostatic radiator valves and electronic thermostatic radiator valves. </p><p>Self-operate thermostatic radiator valves use a wax or liquid-filled element that expands or contracts in response to temperature changes to control the flow of hot water through the radiator. In contrast, electronic thermostatic radiator valves use sensors and electronic controls to regulate the flow of hot water based on the desired temperature set by the user. Both types help in maintaining the desired room temperature efficiently.</p></p>
