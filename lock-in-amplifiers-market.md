<p><p>Lock-in amplifiers are electronic devices that are widely used in measuring systems to extract small signals that are buried in noise. They are specifically designed to lock on to a particular frequency and filter out any other unwanted signals. Lock-in amplifiers consist of three main components: a reference signal source, a phase-sensitive detector, and a low-pass filter. These amplifiers are extensively used in various fields such as physics, chemistry, biology, and material sciences.</p><p>The lock-in amplifiers market is expected to witness substantial growth over the forecast period.
