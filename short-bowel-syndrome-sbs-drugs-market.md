<p><p>Short Bowel Syndrome (SBS) is a rare disorder characterized by the inability of the small intestine to absorb adequate nutrients and fluids. It generally occurs when a significant portion of the small intestine is surgically removed due to conditions like Crohn's disease, tumors, or trauma. This results in malabsorption of essential nutrients, leading to severe diarrhea, dehydration, weight loss, and malnutrition.</p><p>SBS drugs play a crucial role in managing this condition by helping patients to maintain proper nutrient absorption and alleviate symptoms. The market for SBS drugs has been witnessing significant growth due to various factors. Firstly, the increasing prevalence of gastrointestinal diseases and the rising number of surgical interventions lead to a higher incidence of SBS, thereby driving the demand for effective drugs.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in medical technology and the development of innovative drug formulations have also contributed to market growth. For instance, the introduction of GLP-2 analogs such as Gattex and Revestive, which stimulate intestinal growth and improve nutrient absorption, have revolutionized SBS treatment.</p><p>Additionally, the growing focus on research and development activities to introduce novel therapies and drugs is expected to fuel market growth.
