<p><p>Stainless steel is categorized into several types based on its composition and properties. Ferritic stainless steel is known for its high resistance to stress corrosion cracking and is commonly used in automotive exhaust systems. Austenitic stainless steel is highly corrosion-resistant and often used in kitchenware and plumbing. Martensitic stainless steel is strong and tough, often used in knives and blades. PH stainless steel offers exceptional strength and corrosion resistance at high temperatures. Duplex stainless steel combines the benefits of ferritic and austenitic steel. Other stainless steel markets include superaustenitic, precipitation hardening, and high-alloy steels.</p></p>
