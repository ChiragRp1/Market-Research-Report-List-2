<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The current test device market comprises three main types: Clamp Ammeters, Probe Ammeters, and Fork Ammeters. A Clamp Ammeter is a handheld instrument that measures current passing through a wire by clamping around it, making it highly portable and convenient. Probe Ammeters utilize a pointed probe to penetrate insulation and measure current flow in a wire, providing precise readings. Fork Ammeters, on the other hand, are equipped with fork-like jaws that surround the wire, allowing non-contact current measurement. These diverse types cater to different testing needs, providing flexibility and accuracy in current measurement processes.</p></p>
