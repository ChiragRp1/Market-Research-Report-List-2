<p><p>The depression treatment device market encompasses various types of devices used to combat depression. Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation (tDCS) applies a low electrical current to stimulate specific brain areas. Transcranial Alternating Current Stimulation (tACS) uses weak electrical currents to modulate brain oscillations. Transcranial Pulsed Current Stimulation (tPCS) delivers intermittent electrical currents to the brain. Transcranial Random Noise Stimulation (tRNS) applies random electrical frequencies to enhance brain activity. Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT) utilizes electric shocks to induce controlled seizures in the brain. Other devices include magnetic stimulation devices and neurofeedback systems, among others. These devices aim to alleviate symptoms of depression by targeting specific brain regions.</p></p>
