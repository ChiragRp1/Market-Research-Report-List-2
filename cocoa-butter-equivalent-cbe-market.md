<p><p>Cocoa Butter Equivalent (CBE) refers to various fats used as substitutes for cocoa butter in the confectionery and chocolate industry. Shea butter, palm and palm kernel oil, sal fat, kokum butter, and mango butter are some common types of CBEs. Shea butter, derived from the shea tree nut, is known for its moisturizing properties. Palm and palm kernel oil come from palm fruit and are widely used in food products. Sal fat is extracted from the fruit pods of the Sal tree. Kokum butter is obtained from the Garcinia tree, while mango butter is derived from the kernels of mango fruit. These different CBEs serve as alternatives to cocoa butter in various food and cosmetic applications.</p></p>
