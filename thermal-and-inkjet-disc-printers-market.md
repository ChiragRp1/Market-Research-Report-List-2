<p><p>Thermal and Inkjet Disc Printers are two popular types of disc printers used for printing labels directly onto the surface of CD-Rs and DVD-Rs. </p><p>Thermal disc printers, also known as thermal transfer printers, use a heat transfer method to apply ink onto a disc's surface. A thermal print head heats the ink ribbon, and the melted ink is transferred onto the disc through a process of evaporation and condensation. This method provides high-quality and durable prints that are resistant to fading and smudging.</p><p>Inkjet disc printers, on the other hand, use tiny nozzles to spray ink droplets directly onto the disc's surface. This technology offers a wide range of colors and higher resolution prints. Inkjet printers are often more affordable and suitable for short-run or on-demand printing.</p><p>The Thermal and Inkjet Disc Printers Market is witnessing substantial growth, with a projected compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 13.9% during the forecast period.
