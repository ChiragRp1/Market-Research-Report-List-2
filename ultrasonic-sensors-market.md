<p><p>Ultrasonic sensors are used in various industries for detecting objects or measuring distance using sound waves. The ultrasonic retro-reflective sensor emits and receives sound waves from a reflector, enabling detection of objects within a specified range. Ultrasonic proximity sensors emit sound waves and detect their reflection off nearby objects to determine their proximity. Ultrasonic through beam sensors consist of a transmitter and receiver placed opposite to each other, detecting objects when the sound waves are interrupted. These sensors find applications in automation systems, robotics, level measurement, and more.</p></p>
