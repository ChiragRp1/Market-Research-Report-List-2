<p><p>Orally Disintegrating Films (ODFs) are thin, flexible sheets that can be easily dissolved in the mouth without the need for water. There are two main types of ODFs in the market. The first type is Orodispersible Films, which are used for delivering drugs that are absorbed through the oral mucosa and provide fast onset of action. The second type is Oromucosal Films, which are designed for local drug delivery to the mucous membranes of the mouth, offering targeted therapy for conditions like oral infections or inflammation. Both types provide convenient and effective drug administration for patients.</p></p>
