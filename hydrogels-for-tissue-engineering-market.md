<p><p>Hydrogels, widely used in tissue engineering, can be divided into two main types: natural hydrogels and synthetic hydrogels. Natural hydrogels are derived from natural sources such as collagen, gelatin, and fibrin. They offer excellent biocompatibility and cell adhesion, mimicking the natural extracellular matrix. Synthetic hydrogels, on the other hand, are chemically synthesized materials like polyethylene glycol, polyvinyl alcohol, and polyacrylamide. These hydrogels can be easily modified to control their mechanical properties, degradation rates, and biofunctionalities, making them versatile for various tissue engineering applications.</p></p>
