<p><ul><li>Indwelling Voice Prosthesis Devices</li><li>Non-Indwelling Voice Prosthesis Devices</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Voice prosthesis devices are commonly used in the treatment of laryngeal cancer or other conditions that result in voice loss. Indwelling voice prosthesis devices are surgically implanted in the patient's tracheoesophageal puncture and remain in place. They offer ease of use and improved voice quality. On the other hand, non-indwelling voice prosthesis devices are inserted into the puncture during voice production and removed afterwards. They require frequent replacement, but allow for easier cleaning and require less initial surgical intervention.</p></p>
