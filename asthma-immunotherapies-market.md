<p><ul><li>Subcutaneous Immunotherapy</li><li>Sublingual Immunotherapy</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Asthma immunotherapies are medical treatments aimed at desensitizing the immune system to allergens that trigger asthma symptoms. There are two main types of asthma immunotherapies currently used in the market: subcutaneous immunotherapy (also known as allergy shots) and sublingual immunotherapy (also known as allergy drops or tablets). Subcutaneous immunotherapy involves injections of allergens under the skin, gradually increasing the dose over time. Sublingual immunotherapy involves placing allergens under the tongue, with doses typically taken daily or a few times a week. Both treatments aim to reduce asthma symptoms and decrease the need for rescue medications.</p></p>
