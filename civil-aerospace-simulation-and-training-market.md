<p><p>The civil aerospace simulation and training market encompasses various types of simulators used for pilot training. Full Flight Simulators (FFS) are highly advanced simulators that replicate the complete flight experience, including motion and visual displays. Flight Training Devices (FTD) are less complex simulators that focus on specific flight tasks and procedures. Fixed Base Simulators (FBS) are static simulators that typically have limited motion capabilities but still provide realistic cockpit environments. These simulators play a crucial role in training pilots and improving aviation safety.</p></p>
