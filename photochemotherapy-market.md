<p><p>Photochemotherapy, also known as PUVA therapy, is a medical treatment that combines the use of a sensitizer drug and ultraviolet (UV) light to treat various skin conditions, particularly psoriasis, eczema, vitiligo, and cutaneous T-cell lymphoma. The sensitizer drug is applied to the affected area of the skin, followed by exposure to UVA or UVB light. The combination of the drug and light therapy helps to control the growth of abnormal skin cells and reduce inflammation.</p><p>The Photochemotherapy Market is expected to witness steady growth during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of skin disorders, such as psoriasis and eczema, is one of the key factors driving market growth. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), around 125 million people worldwide suffer from psoriasis. Additionally, the rising geriatric population, which is more prone to skin conditions, is contributing to the market growth.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements in light therapy devices and the introduction of portable and more efficient phototherapy equipment are further stimulating market growth. These advancements have led to improved treatment outcomes and reduced side effects, attracting a larger patient population.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing preference for non-invasive and non-pharmacological treatment options among patients is boosting the adoption of photochemotherapy. Compared to systemic medications, photochemotherapy offers a targeted approach with minimal systemic side effects.</p><p>However, challenges associated with the therapy, such as skin damage, increased risk of skin cancer, and limited reimbursement coverage, might hinder market growth to some extent.
