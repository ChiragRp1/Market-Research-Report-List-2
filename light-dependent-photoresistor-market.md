<p><p>The light-dependent photoresistor market encompasses two main types: intrinsic and extrinsic photoresistors. Intrinsic photoresistors are made of semiconductor materials, such as silicon or germanium, and their electrical conductivity varies with light intensity. They are widely used in light-sensitive applications such as cameras or automatic lighting systems. Extrinsic photoresistors, on the other hand, consist of semiconductor materials combined with impurities. These impurities enhance their light sensitivity, making them suitable for more precise light measurement and calibration applications in industries like aerospace or scientific research.</p></p>
