<p><p>A knee surgical robot is a technologically advanced medical device used to assist surgeons during knee surgeries, such as knee replacements or arthroscopic procedures. It offers improved precision, control, and accuracy compared to traditional surgical techniques. The robot enables surgeons to plan the surgery in advance, using 3D virtual models of the patient's knee, and then execute the plan with real-time feedback and guidance during the procedure.</p><p>The knee surgical robot market is expected to experience significant growth during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of orthopedic diseases, such as osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis, is a major driving factor for market growth. Additionally, the rising geriatric population, which is more prone to knee-related ailments, further contributes to the market's expansion.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in technology have allowed for the development of more advanced knee surgical robots, which are capable of providing enhanced surgical outcomes. These robots offer improved accuracy, reduced surgical time, and faster patient recovery, leading to increased adoption by healthcare professionals.</p><p>The market is also witnessing a surge in demand due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The postponement of elective surgeries during the pandemic has led to a backlog of patients awaiting knee surgeries, thereby driving the demand for knee surgical robots.</p><p>In terms of trends, the market is seeing a shift towards the development of miniaturized and portable knee surgical robots. These compact devices offer increased maneuverability and accessibility in the operating room, leading to improved surgical outcomes. Additionally, there is a growing focus on the integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning technologies into knee surgical robots, enabling them to learn from past surgeries and make more informed decisions during procedures.</p><p>Overall, the knee surgical robot market is anticipated to grow at a CAGR of 16.40% during the forecast period, driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of knee-related ailments, advancements in technology, and the backlog of surgeries caused by the COVID-19 pandemic.</p></p>
