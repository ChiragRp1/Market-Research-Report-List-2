<p><p>Sleeve labeling is a type of packaging that involves applying a sleeve label onto a product container using heat or stretching techniques. Heat-shrink sleeve labels are made of plastic film that shrink tightly around the container when heated, providing a 360-degree coverage. Stretch sleeve labels, on the other hand, are elastic and can be stretched over the container without the need for heat. Lastly, the "Others" category includes alternative sleeve labeling methods that may not fit into the heat-shrink or stretch labels, such as roll-on labels or wrap-around labels.</p></p>
