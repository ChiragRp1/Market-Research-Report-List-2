<p><p>Mobile ticketing in transportation refers to the process of using mobile devices, such as smartphones, to purchase and display tickets for various modes of transportation. There are several market types in mobile ticketing, including Near Field Communication (NFCs), QR Codes, and Barcodes. NFCs allow devices to communicate by tapping them together, enabling secure ticket transactions. QR Codes are two-dimensional barcodes that can be quickly scanned by smartphones, providing a convenient way to purchase and validate tickets. Barcodes, on the other hand, use a series of parallel lines of varying thickness to represent data, which can also be scanned by smartphones for ticketing purposes.</p></p>
