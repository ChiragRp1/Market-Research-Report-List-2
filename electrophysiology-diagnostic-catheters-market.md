<p><p>Electrophysiology diagnostic catheters are used in the diagnosis and treatment of various heart rhythm disorders. The imaging catheters are equipped with advanced imaging technologies such as fluoroscopy or intracardiac echocardiography to visualize the inside of the heart during procedures. On the other hand, non-imaging catheters do not have imaging capabilities but are used for mapping and ablating abnormal electrical signals in the heart. Both types play a crucial role in guiding electrophysiologists to accurately diagnose and treat heart rhythm disorders.</p></p>
