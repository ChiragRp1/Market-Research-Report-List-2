<p><p>The Aerospace Landing Gear System market includes various types of landing gear used in aircraft. Strut Landing Gear refers to a system with a single leg attached to the bottom of the aircraft. Rocker Landing Gear features a double-legged design that provides better stability during landing and takeoff. Pontoon Landing Gear refers to a system with floats attached to the bottom of the aircraft, suitable for amphibious planes. Framed Landing Gear involves a structure that supports the aircraft's weight, providing stability and control during landings. These variations cater to different aircraft designs and operational requirements.</p></p>
