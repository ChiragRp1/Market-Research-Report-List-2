<p><p>Isobutylene Isoprene Rubber (IIR) is a synthetic rubber that is produced by polymerizing isobutylene and isoprene monomers. There are two main types of IIR in the market: regular IIR and halogenated IIR. Regular IIR is a non-reactive rubber with excellent heat resistance and chemical stability properties, making it ideal for applications in tire inner tubes, seals, and gaskets. Halogenated IIR, on the other hand, is IIR modified by incorporating halogens like chlorine or bromine, enhancing its resistance to heat, chemicals, and weathering, making it suitable for automotive hoses and belts.</p></p>
