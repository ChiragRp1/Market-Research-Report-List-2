<p><p>Unmanned Underwater Vehicles (UUVs) are divided into two main categories: Autonomous Underwater Vehicles (AUVs) and Remotely Operated Vehicles (ROVs). AUVs are self-propelled vehicles that navigate underwater without any direct human control. They are pre-programmed to carry out specific tasks such as conducting surveys or collecting scientific data. On the other hand, ROVs are remotely controlled by human operators using a surface vessel. ROVs are connected to the operator through a cable, and they are commonly used for tasks that require more precise control and manipulation underwater, such as conducting underwater inspections or performing maintenance tasks.</p></p>
