<p><p>Dry white wine market consists of two main types: still wines and sparkling wines. Still wines are made by fermenting grape juice without carbonation, resulting in a non-effervescent product. They have a crisp and refreshing taste and are typically served chilled. Sparkling wines, on the other hand, undergo a secondary fermentation process to produce carbonation, creating bubbles and a fizzy texture. They are often associated with celebrations and can range from dry to sweet in taste, offering a delightful effervescence when consumed. Both types cater to different preferences and occasions within the wine market.</p></p>
