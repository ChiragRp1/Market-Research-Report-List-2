<p><p>The aluminum cookware market consists of two main types: cast aluminum cookware and anodized aluminum cookware. Cast aluminum cookware is made by pouring molten aluminum into a mold and allowing it to cool and solidify. This type of cookware is known for its excellent heat distribution and durability. On the other hand, anodized aluminum cookware is created by applying an electrochemical process to aluminum, resulting in a hard, non-stick surface that is resistant to corrosion. Both types of cookware are popular choices for cooking due to their light weight and ability to conduct heat efficiently.</p></p>
