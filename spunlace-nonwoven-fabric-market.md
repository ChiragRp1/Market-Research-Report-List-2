<p><p>Spunlace nonwoven fabric is a type of fabric made by entangling fibers using high-pressure water jets. Two common types of spunlace fabric are parallel lapped and cross lapped. In parallel lapped fabric, the fibers are arranged in parallel layers, resulting in a uniform and smooth finish. Cross lapped fabric, on the other hand, involves layering fibers in a cross direction, enhancing its strength and absorbency. Both types have different applications based on their properties, such as filtration, wipes, medical products, and clothing.</p></p>
