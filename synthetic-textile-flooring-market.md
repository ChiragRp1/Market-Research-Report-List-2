<p><p>The synthetic textile flooring market includes three main types: tufting, woven, and needlefelt. Tufting is a popular method where yarn is punched through a backing fabric to create loops or cut piles. Woven flooring is produced by interlacing yarns vertically and horizontally, resulting in a strong and durable product. Needlefelt flooring uses barbed needles to entangle synthetic fibers together, creating a dense and sturdy surface. Each type offers unique characteristics, such as tufting's versatility, woven's strength, and needlefelt's durability, catering to different consumer preferences.</p></p>
